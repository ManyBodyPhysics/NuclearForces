Heavy Baryons Lagrangian

Consider now pion-exchange contributions to the potential $$ \begin{equation*} V_{\pi} = V_{1\pi} + V_{2\pi} + V_{3\pi} + \ldots \,, \end{equation*} $$ where one-, two- and three-pion exchange (3PE) contributions \( V_{1\pi} \), \( V_{2\pi} \) and \( V_{3\pi} \) can be written in the low-momentum expansion as $$ \begin{align*} \tag{1} V_{1\pi} &= V_{1\pi}^{(0)} + V_{1\pi}^{(2)} + V_{1\pi}^{(3)} + V_{1\pi}^{(4)} +\ldots \\ V_{2\pi} &= V_{2\pi}^{(2)} + V_{2\pi}^{(3)} + V_{2\pi}^{(4)} + \ldots \\ V_{3\pi} &= V_{3\pi}^{(4)} + \ldots \,. \end{align*} $$ Here, the superscripts denote the corresponding chiral order and the ellipses refer to \( (Q/\Lambda)^5 \)- and higher order terms. Contributions due to the exchange of four- and more pions are further suppressed: \( n \)--pion exchange diagrams start to contribute at the order \( (Q/\Lambda)^{2n-2} \). Notice further that in addition to isopin-invariant contributions there are isospin–breaking corrections.

The static 1PE potential at N3LO has the form $$ \begin{equation*} V_{1\pi}^{(0)} + V_{1\pi}^{(2)} + V_{1\pi}^{(3)} + V_{1\pi}^{(4)} = -\biggl(\frac{g_A}{2F_\pi}\biggr)^2 \, ( 1 + \delta )^2 \, \tau_1 \cdot \tau_2 \, \frac{\vec{\sigma}_1 \cdot\vec{q}\,\vec{\sigma}_2\cdot\vec{q}} {\vec q \, ^2 + M_\pi^2}\,. \end{equation*} $$

The 2PE contributions are convenient to express as \( V_{2 \pi} \) in the form: $$ \begin{align*} V_{2 \pi} &= V_C + \tau_1 \cdot \tau_2 \, W_C + \left[ V_S + \tau_1 \cdot \tau_2 \, W_S \right] \, \vec \sigma_1 \cdot \vec \sigma_2 + \left[ V_T + \tau_1 \cdot \tau_2 \, W_T \right] \, \vec \sigma_1 \cdot \vec q \, \vec \sigma_2 \cdot \vec q \\ &+ \left[ V_{LS} + \tau_1 \cdot \tau_2 \, W_{LS} \right] \, i ( \vec \sigma_1 + \vec \sigma_2 ) \cdot ( \vec q \times \vec k ) \\ &+ \left[ V_{\sigma L} + \tau_1 \cdot \tau_2 \, W_{\sigma L} \right] \, \vec \sigma_1 \cdot (\vec q \times \vec k ) \vec \sigma_2 \cdot (\vec q \times \vec k ) \,, \nonumber \end{align*} $$ where the superscripts \( C \), \( S \), \( T \), \( LS \) and \( \sigma L \) of the scalar functions \( V_C \), \( \ldots \), \( W_{\sigma L} \) refer to central, spin-spin, tensor, spin-orbit and quadratic spin-orbit components, respectively.
