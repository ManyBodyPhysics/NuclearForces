Effective Chiral Lagrangian

The starting point for the derivation of the \( NN \) interaction is an effective chiral Lagrangian $$ {\cal L}={\cal L}_{\pi N}+{\cal L}_{\pi\pi}+{\cal L}_{NN}, $$ which is given by a series of terms of increasing chiral dimension, $$ {\cal L}_{\pi N} = {\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(1)}+ {\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}+{\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(3)} + \ldots , $$ $$ {\cal L}_{\pi\pi}={\cal L}_{\pi\pi}^{(2)}+ \ldots , $$ $$ {\cal L}_{NN} ={\cal L}_{NN}^{(0)}+{\cal L}_{NN}^{(2)}+{\cal L}_{NN}^{(4)} + \ldots, $$ where the superscript refers to the number of derivatives or pion mass insertions (chiral dimension). Good review: Epelbaum, Prog. Part. Nucl. Phys. 57, 654 (2006).
